Casting Characters include the Sorceress, Necromancer, Elemental Druid, and Traps Assassin.

There are many options for Caster Characters. Although this page has suggestions about popular items, there is no perfect guide to the best items to wear. Generally, it is best to rate items against each other and select the ones that best help your character. This page mostly lists the Ultimate Items rather than listing all options available. Check out the Item Section for a more complete list of what is available. Class-specific Casting Character equipment is not mentioned on this page.

Basic Magic Ability List

+ All Skill Levels, + All Skill Tabs, + Individual Skills
Skill Levels, Mana Cost, and Skill Damage are very important to spell casters. By increasing Skill Levels, players can reduce Mana usage and reduce the amount of time required to kill monsters as a result of increased spell damage. Many players choose to make increasing Skill Levels their number one priority.

+ Mana
As you add more Mana you can cast spells more times and you can increase your Mana Regeneration. For more information about Mana Regeneration refer to the Mana Regeneration Formula.

Regenerate Mana %
This will increase the rate your Mana is regenerated.

Maximum Mana %
Whenever possible try to collect items that increase % Maximum Mana because they will provide a significant increase in total Mana and increase your Mana Regeneration. Here are some items:

Damage Taken Goes To Mana
This can generate extra Mana as a result of being attacked. It does not reduce the amount of damage to Life but instead just adds to Mana.

Faster Cast Rate
Cast Rate items will reduce the time it takes to cast spells. Cast Rate items stack so the more you put on, the faster you can cast spells. While you may not want to sacrifice to wear an item with Faster Cast Rate, it certainly helps to have it on any items you wear. Faster Cast Rate Items will not improve casting speed of Assassin Traps. Faster Cast Items will not help skills with Casting Delays.

+ Life
Casters are generally very weak against melee attacks which means additional Life is important to their survival. Players can place points in Vitality to increase Life and also use Items to add additional Life.

Resistances
It is best to try to maximize Resistances, especially Lightning, Fire and Cold.

Magic Find
Magic Find generally comes at a price. For more tips on Magic Find Equipment go here.

Charms
Good Charms to have are + Life, + Skills, and + Resistances.

Hit Recovery
Items with Hit Recovery allow you to quickly recover after having been hit. Without Hit Recovery items, when a character is hit, it will be stunned and will simply stand in place until able to move again. If multiple monsters are attacking at once, the character may remain continually stunned until it dies. But, with Hit Recovery items characters can quickly return to battle after being hit. Hit Recovery items stack, so they can be used on several different pieces of equipment (belts, armor, boots or, shields) significantly reducing the length of time a character remains stunned.

Crafted Items
There are several Caster Crafted Items you can create here.

Second Weapon Slot
You can use your second Weapon Slots for Weapons and Shields with high Resistances, in case players encounter a boss with a number of elemental attacks. You can also place Weapons, Staves, Orbs, Wands that give bonuses to skills that you do not have skill points in but still wish to use. Examples of such spells would be Frozen Armor or Enchant. Another option is to use a Wand that has charges of Lower Resist. You can switch to the alternative weapon slot, cast the spell, then return to your main weapon slot when necessary.

Boots
Look for Boots that offer Defense and useful magical bonuses such as Faster Run/Walk, Faster Hit Recovery, Resistances or additional Life/Mana (if needed). Boots that give a Faster Run/Walk Speed, and faster Stamina Regeneration can really help characters avoid danger and reach their destination much quicker.

Initially, look for boots that reduce Stamina drain or offer additional Stamina. Boots that Heal Stamina 50% will heal Stamina faster when characters stop. Vidala's Fetlock will give 150 additional Stamina. Treads of Cthon will take 50% less Stamina when characters run and walk which means in essence that it doubles Stamina. If Stamina doubled is greater than Stamina +150, then Treads of Cthon will allow a character to run longer than Vidala's.

As Characters accrue more vitality, they can eventually run as often as needed (within reason) and not run out of Stamina. At that point, Stamina reducing boots are no longer needed and should be replaced with "Faster Run/Walk." This will free up Magical Prefix/Suffixes on your boots.
